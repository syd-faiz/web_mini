AMD Ryzen 9 3990X Vs Intel Core i9-9900K:
Which is a better CPU?

The Intel Core i9-9900K and the AMD Ryzen 9 3990X are both high-end processors aimed at demanding workloads, but they have some significant differences.

1) Core Count and Thread Count: The AMD Ryzen 9 3990X has a massive 64 cores and 128 threads, while the Intel Core i9-9900K has only 8 cores and 16 threads. This means the Ryzen 9 3990X can handle much more processing power than the i9-9900K.

2) Clock Speeds: The Core i9-9900K has a base clock speed of 3.6GHz and can turbo boost up to 5.0GHz. On the other hand, the Ryzen 9 3990X has a base clock speed of 2.9GHz and can turbo boost up to 4.3GHz. This means the i9-9900K is faster at single-threaded tasks, while the Ryzen 9 3990X is faster at multi-threaded tasks.

3) Power Consumption: The Core i9-9900K has a TDP (Thermal Design Power) of 95 watts, while the Ryzen 9 3990X has a TDP of 280 watts. This means the Ryzen 9 3990X consumes significantly more power than the i9-9900K.

4) Price: The Ryzen 9 3990X is a much more expensive processor than the Core i9-9900K. As of 2021, the Ryzen 9 3990X has a retail price of around $3,999, while the Core i9-9900K has a retail price of around $500.


Overall, the AMD Ryzen 9 3990X is a much more powerful processor than the Intel Core i9-9900K, but it is also much more expensive and consumes more power. The i9-9900K may be a better choice for users who need a high-performance CPU for single-threaded applications, while the Ryzen 9 3990X is a better choice for users who need the most processing power for multi-threaded applications.
